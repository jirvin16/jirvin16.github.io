Research

Here are some my research projects at Stanford and UCSB.

CheXNeXt - Deep learning for chest radiograph diagnosis

Project with Pranav Rajpurkar and Professor Matt Lungren, Professor Curt Langlotz, Professor Andrew Ng

We developed a model (CheXNeXt) to concurrently detect 14 clinically important diseases in chest radiographs. We demonstrated that the model achieved performance comparable to that of radiologists on 11 of the 14 diseases.

Project WebpagePaper (PLOS Medicine)

MRNet - Deep-learning-assisted diagnosis for knee magnetic resonance imaging

Project with Pranav Rajpurkar and Professor Matt Lungren, Professor Curt Langlotz, Professor Andrew Ng

We developed a model (MRNet) to predict abnormalities in knee MRI exams, and measured the clinical utility of providing the model's predictions to radiologists and surgeons during interpretation. We found that model assistance significantly reduced the rate at which healthy patients would be mistakenly diagnosed as having ACL tears.

Project WebpagePaper (PLOS Medicine)

MURA - Large Dataset for Abnormality Detection in Musculoskeletal Radiographs

Project with Pranav Rajpurkar and Professor Matt Lungren, Professor Curt Langlotz, Professor Andrew Ng

We developed a dataset (MURA) to serve as a benchmark for the advancement of research in medical imaging. MURA contains over 40,000 X-ray images of the upper extremeties which were manually annotated by board-certified radiologists as normal or abnormal.

Project WebpagePaper (MIDL 2018)

ChexNet - Radiologist-Level Pneumonia Detection

Project with Pranav Rajpurkar and Professor Matt Lungren, Professor Curt Langlotz, Professor Andrew Ng

We developed a model (CheXNet) that can detect pneumonia from chest radiographs at the level of practicing radiologists. Our model is a 121-layer convolutional neural network trained on ChestX-ray14, the largest publicly available chest X-ray dataset.

Project WebpagePaper (ARXIV)MIT Tech Review
